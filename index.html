Why Low Earth Orbit?

Low Earth Orbit (LEO) is the perfect destination for space tourism, offering a unique blend of accessibility, breathtaking views, and safety. Located just above Earth’s atmosphere, LEO provides a relatively short distance for travel, ensuring a quicker and more comfortable journey. From this vantage point, guests can witness the curvature of the Earth, vibrant sunrises and sunsets, and the mesmerizing dance of stars. Moreover, LEO’s proximity allows for a safe and swift return to Earth, making it an ideal choice for those seeking an unforgettable space experience without extended stays.



Business Model Design



Economic Feasibility for LEO Space Hotel:
• Primary Revenue: High-end orbital stays ($5M–$30M per guest).
• Secondary: Leasing 20% volume to research/media clients.
• Target Market: Ultra-High-Net-Worth Individuals & corporates.
• Cost Control: Fully reusable launch systems & long-life modules.
• Key Partners: Launch provider, life-support experts, hotel brands.



Prototype Design


Prototype Concept: Variable-Gravity Recreation Module
• Rotating section (~3 RPM) creates 0.5g artificial gravity.
• At 650km altitude (least drag and radiation)
• Reduces nausea & muscle loss during multi-day stays.
• Includes airlock, ECLSS (98% water/oxygen recycle), onboard medical bay and sound-proof cabins.
• Digital training simulator for guest safety and fire/airlock operation.



Operational Challenges & Mitigation


Main Risks & Countermeasures:
• Passenger Safety: Medical screening, redundant systems, NASA-level standards.
Safety Precaution: Prove the shuttle can survive travel, orbit, and safe return.
• Logistics: Monthly cargo launches (JIT model) for consumables.
• Psychological Health: Earth-view windows, internet, recreation zone.
• Regulatory: FAA-AST licensing + international liability compliance.
