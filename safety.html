Safety & FAQs

Your guide to a secure and unforgettable journey to low earth orbit. We've compiled answers to your most pressing questions.

Do I need astronaut training?

No, you do not need astronaut training. Our orbital hotel stays are designed for space tourists, not astronauts. We provide comprehensive pre-flight briefings and in-flight support to ensure your safety and comfort. Our experienced crew will guide you through all necessary procedures, making your journey accessible and enjoyable for everyone.

How safe is space travel?

Space travel has an exceptionally high safety record due to rigorous preparation and redundancy systems. Our vehicles utilize the latest aerospace technology, and all crew members are highly trained professionals. Safety is our non-negotiable priority, and we adhere to and exceed global space safety standards.

What about radiation shielding?

Our habitat is engineered with multi-layer shielding materials designed to mitigate space radiation exposure. The orbital path is strategically chosen to minimize time spent in high-risk zones like the Van Allen belts. Continuous monitoring is performed to ensure guest safety throughout the stay.

What medical training is required?

Guests undergo a basic medical screening prior to booking to ensure fitness for space travel. No specialized medical training is required. Our onboard medical officer is a fully trained physician equipped to handle emergencies and general health concerns.

Are there safety drills?

Yes, all guests participate in mandatory, brief safety and emergency procedure briefings before launch and upon arrival at the orbital hotel. These cover crucial information like emergency depressurization and evacuation procedures.
